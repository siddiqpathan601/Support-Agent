# Aspects in Astrology

Aspects are angular relationships between planets in a birth chart. They describe how planetary energies interact — whether they support each other, create tension, or blend in complex ways. Aspects are one of the most important factors in chart interpretation.

## Major Aspects

### Conjunction (0°) — Fusion
**Orb:** 8-10°  
**Nature:** Neutral (depends on planets involved)  
A conjunction merges the energies of two planets into a single, intensified force. The planets operate together as a unit, amplifying each other's qualities. Conjunctions are the most powerful aspect — they concentrate energy in one sign and house area.

**Example:** Sun conjunct Mars creates a person with powerful vitality, assertiveness, and drive. The identity (Sun) and action (Mars) are fused.

### Opposition (180°) — Awareness Through Polarity
**Orb:** 6-8°  
**Nature:** Challenging but awareness-building  
An opposition places two planets directly across the zodiac from each other, creating a tug-of-war dynamic. It brings awareness through contrast and requires integration of opposing needs. Oppositions often manifest in relationship dynamics.

**Example:** Moon opposite Saturn creates tension between emotional needs (Moon) and duty/restriction (Saturn) — learning to balance nurturing with responsibility.

### Trine (120°) — Flow and Harmony
**Orb:** 6-8°  
**Nature:** Harmonious  
A trine connects planets in signs of the same element, creating a natural, easy flow of energy. Trines represent talents, gifts, and areas where things come naturally. The risk is complacency — trines can be taken for granted.

**Example:** Venus trine Jupiter brings natural charm, generosity, and good fortune in relationships and finances.

### Square (90°) — Tension and Growth
**Orb:** 6-8°  
**Nature:** Challenging  
A square creates dynamic tension between planets that demand action and resolution. Squares are the most growth-producing aspects — they push you to overcome obstacles and develop strength. They are uncomfortable but powerful.

**Example:** Mars square Pluto creates intense willpower, power struggles, and transformative drive — a demanding but ultimately empowering aspect.

### Sextile (60°) — Opportunity
**Orb:** 4-6°  
**Nature:** Harmonious  
A sextile connects planets in compatible signs, creating opportunities that require some effort to activate. Sextiles represent talents that need conscious development — they offer doors that you must choose to walk through.

**Example:** Mercury sextile Uranus brings an innovative, quick mind with flashes of insight — but requires cultivation.

## Minor Aspects

### Quincunx / Inconjunct (150°)
**Orb:** 2-3°  
Creates an awkward, adjusting energy between planets with nothing in common by sign. Requires constant adaptation and health awareness.

### Semi-sextile (30°)
**Orb:** 2°  
A subtle, mildly irritating connection between adjacent signs. Creates a need for small adjustments.

### Semi-square (45°) and Sesquiquadrate (135°)
**Orb:** 2°  
Minor stress aspects creating friction and irritation. Less intense than squares but persistently uncomfortable.

## Aspect Patterns

### Grand Trine
Three planets forming trines to each other, creating a triangle of ease in one element. Represents great talent but potential for complacency.

### T-Square
Two planets in opposition with a third squaring both, creating a focal point of tension and drive. Highly motivating but stressful.

### Grand Cross
Four planets forming two oppositions and four squares. An intense, high-energy configuration demanding balance across four life areas.

### Yod (Finger of God)
Two planets in sextile with both quincunx a third. Creates a sense of fated mission or special purpose requiring constant adjustment.

## Transit Aspects
When a moving planet forms an aspect to a natal planet, it activates that natal planet's themes. The nature of the aspect determines whether the activation is harmonious (trine, sextile) or challenging (square, opposition). Conjunctions intensify, and their effect depends on the transiting planet's nature.
